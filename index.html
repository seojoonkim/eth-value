Valuation Methodology
Reference
TVL Multiple ★★★★☆
Price = TVL × 7 ÷ Supply
DeFi ecosystem value-based. Higher TVL indicates greater network utility. 7x multiple derived from historical averages.
Popularity: High Reliability: Medium-High
⚠️ TVL may include leverage and double-counting
Staking Scarcity ★★★☆☆
Price × √(Supply ÷ Liquid)
Applies scarcity premium when circulating supply decreases due to staking. Square root function dampens volatility.
Popularity: Low Reliability: Medium
⚠️ Proprietary model. Lacks academic validation
MC/TVL Fair Value ★★★★☆
Price × (6 ÷ Current Ratio)
Assumes MC/TVL ratio reverts to historical average (6x). Similar to P/E relative valuation.
Popularity: High Reliability: Medium-High
✓ Widely used industry comparison metric
Metcalfe's Law ★★★★★
2 × (TVL/1B)^1.5 × 1B ÷ Supply
Network value proportional to square of users/activity. Validated model in Bitcoin/Ethereum analysis.
Popularity: Very High Reliability: High
✓ Network effect model backed by academic research
DCF (Staking Yield) ★★★☆☆
P × 1.035 ÷ (0.10 - 0.03)
Traditional DCF model applied. Uses staking yield (3.5%) as cash flow, 10% discount rate and 3% growth rate.
Popularity: Medium Reliability: Medium
⚠️ Sensitive to discount/growth rate assumptions
BTC Ratio Model ★★★☆☆
BTC_MCap × 0.45 ÷ Supply
Assumes ETH reaches 45% of BTC market cap (currently ~35%). Related to "flippening" narrative.
Popularity: High Reliability: Low
⚠️ 45% target ratio is subjective assumption
L2 Ecosystem ★★★☆☆
(TVL + L2_TVL×2) × 6 ÷ Supply
Combines L1 TVL with weighted L2 TVL (2x). Based on thesis that L2 value accrues to L1.
Popularity: Low Reliability: Medium
⚠️ L2 weight (2x) is proprietary assumption
P/E Ratio (25x) ★★★★☆
Annual_Fees × 25 ÷ Supply
Treats network fees as "earnings" and applies tech stock average P/E (25x). Used by Token Terminal.
Popularity: High Reliability: Medium-High
✓ Comparable to traditional finance metrics
⚠️ Disclaimer: These valuation models are for reference only. Each model has its own assumptions and limitations. "Popularity" refers to usage frequency in the industry/community. "Reliability" indicates the level of academic backing and empirical validation. Investment decisions should consider multiple factors comprehensively.
